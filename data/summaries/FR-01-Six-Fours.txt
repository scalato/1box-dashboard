Occupancy by unit count recovered modestly in April with a net gain of 6 units, then stabilized in May with zero net movement, though the overall trend across the three months shows minimal change at 84.0% to 85.3%. Rental velocity weakened notably in May, with move-ins dropping to 18 against 25–28 in the prior two months, resulting in flat net rentals despite matching vacates. Revenue per square meter improved steadily from 20.56 to 21.49, offsetting the slight occupancy decline and driving net revenue growth of roughly 1% month-over-month in May. Arrears remain a concern, rising sharply from 58,757 in April to 64,009 in May with 27 accounts in default, up from a low of 17 in April.
