Occupancy declined over the three-month period, falling from 80.6% to 79.6% by unit count and from 83.7% to 82.6% by square meter, driven by a sharp reversal in June when the site posted net negative rentals of minus 7 units after two months of modest gains. Rental velocity weakened significantly, with move-ins dropping from 21 in April to just 7 in June, while vacates remained elevated at 14 units in the final month. Revenue held relatively stable at approximately 53,000 euros despite occupancy pressure, supported by a rising average rate per square meter that increased from 15.87 to 16.38. Arrears improved notably, declining from 7,936 euros across 58 accounts in April to 7,045 euros across 45 accounts in June, suggesting better collection performance.
