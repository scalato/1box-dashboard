Occupancy by square meter improved steadily from 82.2% in April to 86.5% in June, while unit occupancy recovered to 83.1% after dipping to 81.4% in May. Rental velocity was volatile, with net losses of 2 units in May offset by modest net gains of 3 units in June, resulting in a net addition of 1 unit across the three-month period. Revenue remained relatively flat around €18,500 monthly despite occupancy gains, as average rate per square meter declined from €14.68 to €14.56. Arrears improved significantly from €2,645.94 in April to €1,269.13 in June, reducing the number of accounts in arrears from 20 to 16 over the same span.
