Occupancy by square meter improved steadily from 81.3% in March to 85.1% in May, a gain of 3.8 percentage points, while unit occupancy peaked in April at 82.5% then softened slightly to 81.4% in May. Rental velocity was mixed: the site recovered from a -9 unit net decline in March with +7 units added in April, but returned to a -2 unit loss in May as vacates slightly exceeded move-ins. Revenue grew modestly from €18,024 to €18,509 across the period, though the per-square-meter rate dipped in May to €14.24 from €14.68 in April. Arrears spiked to €2,646 across 20 accounts in April but fell sharply to €883 across 10 accounts by May, suggesting improved collections.
