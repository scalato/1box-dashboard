Occupancy by unit count peaked in May at 78.0% before declining to 77.2% in June, while square-meter occupancy slipped from 81.4% to 80.8% over the same period. Rental velocity turned negative in June with a net loss of 5 units after a strong May that added 20 units, indicating softening demand. Revenue remained relatively flat around €63,000 monthly despite the occupancy dip, buoyed by rate increases from €17.88 to €18.65 per square meter. Arrears outstanding grew steadily from €4,681 to €7,037 across the three-month window, though the number of accounts in arrears fell from 59 to 33, suggesting concentration among fewer tenants with larger balances.
