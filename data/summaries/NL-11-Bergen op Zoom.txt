Occupancy improved significantly in May, with unit occupancy rising from 63.4% in April to 65.8% and square-meter occupancy jumping to 74.6% from 69.2%, driven by a net gain of 6 units after two months of losses. Rental velocity turned positive in May with only one vacate against 7 move-ins, reversing the negative trend seen in March and April. Revenue held relatively flat around 21,400 across the period despite the occupancy gains, as the average rate per square meter declined from 17.18 to 16.41. Arrears count improved to 15 accounts in May from 20, though the outstanding balance increased to 3,736, suggesting concentration among fewer tenants.
