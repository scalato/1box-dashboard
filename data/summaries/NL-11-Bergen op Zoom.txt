Occupancy has improved steadily over the three-month window, with unit occupancy rising from 63.4% in April to 66.9% in June, driven by net gains of 6 units in May and 3 units in June after an initial loss of 3 units in April. Revenue climbed 5.1% from April to June (€21,390 to €22,481), though average rent per sqm dipped slightly from €16.88 to €16.51, suggesting the gains came from volume rather than rate increases. Arrears have become a concern, more than doubling from €2,043 across 20 accounts in April to €4,555 across 33 accounts by June, indicating deteriorating payment compliance despite occupancy growth.
