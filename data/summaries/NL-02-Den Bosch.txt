Occupancy declined modestly over the three-month period, dropping from 79.5% to 78.8% on a square-meter basis, with unit occupancy holding steady at 76.2% since May after a 0.6-point dip. Rental velocity has been consistently negative, with net losses of 2 units in April and 3 units in May before stabilizing at zero net rentals in June, reflecting weak move-in activity that declined from 16 units in April to just 8 in June. Revenue remained relatively flat across the span, rising slightly from 48,472 to 49,151, while the average rate per square meter improved to 20.52 in June. Arrears outstanding nearly tripled from 2,183 to 5,521 despite the count of accounts in arrears declining from 26 to 15, signaling that fewer customers owe money but those who do owe significantly more.
