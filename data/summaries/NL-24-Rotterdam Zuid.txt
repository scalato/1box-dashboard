Occupancy has declined steadily over the three-month window, with unit occupancy falling from 86.5% in April to 83.9% in June, driven by net losses of 8 and 10 units in May and June respectively. Rental velocity has weakened, with vacates consistently outpacing move-ins each month, particularly in June when 24 units were vacated against 15 move-ins. Revenue per square meter has increased from €23.04 to €24.32, but this pricing lift has not offset the occupancy erosion—net revenue grew only modestly despite the declining unit base. Arrears remain elevated and volatile, rising to €27,097.51 across 98 accounts in June after a dip in May, signaling both collection pressure and tenant quality concerns.
