Occupancy has remained relatively flat over the three-month period, declining slightly from 86.1% (unit) in March to 86.5% in May, though square-meter occupancy dropped 0.6 points to 87.7%. Rental velocity improved markedly, shifting from net minus 11 units in March to breakeven in April and net positive 4 units in May, indicating stabilization and modest recovery. Revenue climbed 2.2% from March to May (€58,391 to €59,683), driven by consistent rate increases averaging 21.88 per sqm by May. Arrears have deteriorated significantly, rising from €1,394 across 38 accounts in March to €3,880 across 20 accounts by May, signaling a concentration of outstanding debt despite the improvement in arrears count.
