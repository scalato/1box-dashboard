Occupancy by square metre declined steadily from 81.2% in April to 78.8% in June, a loss of 2.4 percentage points over the quarter, though unit occupancy remained relatively flat at around 79%. Rental velocity improved modestly, moving from net negative two units in April to net positive three units in June as move-ins accelerated to 21 in the latest month. Revenue declined approximately 3% across the period from €31,940 to €31,011 as rate-per-sqm softened slightly from €21.32 to €21.34, suggesting downward pressure on pricing despite higher leasing activity. Arrears spiked to €5,108 across 34 accounts in May but recovered to €2,769 across 21 accounts in June, indicating temporary payment issues that have since normalised.
